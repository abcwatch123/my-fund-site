Year 0 Letter

Value allocation

People often ask me how it is I do what I do, or why it is I do what I do. So I might as well try to distill my thoughts in one place. The first thing to acknowledge is that there are many people better at this than me. They've been doing it for longer and work much harder. I've been doing this for a short time and this is just my experience so far.

More for less

I started investing on the side while I had a full time job. Things changed when I started making more money investing than at my job. I don't believe in side hustles. I believe that if you’re good at something and truly believe in it, you should make it your main hustle. Call it laziness or efficiency, but I'm obsessed with maximising reward for the least amount of effort.

Price / value

The real skill in investing is in price value. Everyone around you might be saying something is worth a certain price. It's your job to think critically based on first principles to discern what its true value is.

You have to be both contrarian and right. It's easy to be contrarian (by disagreeing with everything). And it’s easy to be right (by agreeing with obvious things). But it’s very difficult to be both contrarian and right.

Growing up, my dad used to tell me that nowadays people know the price of everything and the value of nothing. He was making a point about materialism and what really matters in life. But it's applicable to investing too. And in many ways, my full time job is finding and capitalising on the difference between price and value.

The underdog

It's also the story of believing in a company when nobody else does. Rooting for the underdog. This speaks deeply to me because I can personally relate to it. I did not do well as a kid in the school system. I scraped by with my exams and most of my teachers disliked me. But the teachers and people that did believe in me, I remember forever.

Venturing into the unknown is what it takes to obtain true reward. Not being comfortable in what's known. It applies to more than investing. We were talking about having kids last night and that's another great example of venturing into the unknown. It's hopeless to try and plan every detail out because you just don't know what's coming.

It's a call to an adventure and you have to risk what you have in order to experience what is to come. We all have to spend our lives on something. We have to spend ourselves on something. And there are few things more beautiful than spending your life on your children.

Pain and courage

Hard work pays off. More importantly, hard work with courage pays off. Courage to act on an opportunity. Courage to act when people might be telling you it's wrong, but you know it's right. Find something hard that no one else wants to do, be courageous, and do it. It will be painful, but worth it.

Ayrton Senna said, “If you no longer go for a gap that exists, you're no longer a racing driver.” I think about this a lot. And it certainly applies to life beyond racing. Have the courage to go for the gap.
